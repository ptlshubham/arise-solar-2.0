Utility Rebate/Grant Management

Although the price of solar panels has decreased substantially over the past decade, it's still a significant investment for businesses and home homeowners looking to reduce their electricity costs. To encourage increased use of solar governments across the country, the state and local government, and some utilities also offer incentives that make installing solar panels on your roof as well as making your home energy efficient by renewable power more cost-effective and affordable. These savings in energy typically come in the form of rebates for solar as well as tax incentives or incentives based on performance and could reduce the price of solar power by up to 50 percent! Grants are funds that are used to encourage renewable energy sources that do not require payment back. These groups are those with low incomes, farmers, rural homeowners, Native American tribes, schools, and non-profit groups.

Utilizing solar energy - despite offering many long-term benefits, can be a costly undertaking. This guide will aid homeowners in the present and the future to learn about Federal financing options that are available to them. It is important to note that the U.S. government is committed to renewable energy initiatives and for good reason. A few of the ways that it invests to benefit the United States as a whole are:

  • Removing the use of fossil fuels that are finite and assisting the nation to be energy independent
  • Enhancing the environmental quality by cutting CO2 emissions
  • harnessing the unlimited quality of sunlight energy
  • The creation of more jobs in energy efficiency-focused industries.
  • Making partnerships to increase chances for renewable power sources in federally assisted housing
  • Increasing the number of megawatts from renewable energy

The federal tax credit for investment (ITC) is without doubt the best solar incentive. The ITC provides 30 percent of the amount you spent on the solar installation on federal income taxes. In lieu of deducting that reduces your taxable income (as is the case with all charitable contributions you make throughout an entire year) A tax credit directly offsets the amount you'd otherwise be owed in taxes. Also, instead of being taxed on a lower amount of income, the federal ITC reduces the amount you owe in taxes. It may even return to you as a tax refund to the IRS.
