The environmental benefits of solar Energy

Solar power advocates frequently speak about how going solar will help the planet. However, they might not be able to explain the advantages of solar power in depth. Ultimately, you could ask, "Are solar panels eco-friendly?"

Are thinking of installing an energy source from solar at home, at work, or in your local community? Let's look at the ways that solar photovoltaic (PV) systems affect the environment, and why solar power is green.

Solar Energy's Health and Environmental Benefits

Going solar and ditching fossil fuels can have a great impact on the environment and your wellbeing. To help you spread the word about solar energy, here are four major benefits of solar power for the environment.

1. Reduced Air Pollution

One of the greatest advantages of solar energy is that it creates only a few air pollutants. The National Renewable Energy Laboratory found that if solar power is widely adopted. It could substantially reduce harmful emissions like sulfur dioxide, nitrous oxides, and particulate matter that pose health risks. This means fewer cases of chronic bronchitis, cardiovascular and respiratory issues. It a fewer missed workdays due to health problems.

2. Combats Global Warming

Carbon emissions as well as other greenhouse gases are among the major factors in the rise in temperature across the globe. This emissions are referred to as global warming. While some greenhouse gas emissions could be present in transportation and manufacturing, solar photovoltaic panels do not emit greenhouse gases when they generate electricity, making them an excellent renewable energy source.

As a result, every kilowatt-hour of solar energy produced instead of fossil fuel like natural gas could reduce carbon emissions. By reducing greenhouse gas emissions and the use of solar energy, global warming can be reduced.

3. Reduces Resource Stress

It's easy to overlook the fact that there is only a limited quantity of fossil fuels that are available and once they're gone, it is impossible to be able to get them back (at least for a long time). So, switching towards renewable power isn't only greener, it's essential to ensure that our world is powered for the next generation.

Alongside the limited supply of fossil fuels used as energy sources water is another vital resource to be considered for the long-term health of our planet. While the process of making solar panels requires some water (especially for mining and the processing process of minerals) , the process makes use of much less water than traditional energy sources.

4. Improves Global Health

Beyond its impact in causing global warming, carbon dioxide emissions may cause serious harm to human health, impacting vital organs such as the lungs and heart. By reducing the amount of carbon dioxide and other harmful substances entering the atmosphere, the cleaner air created through solar energy can lower the risk of asthma attacks, heart disease attacks, and premature deaths which are health concerns that affect millions worldwide.
