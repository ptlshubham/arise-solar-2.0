Why are solar Panels installed at an Angle?

Solar panels are most effective when they receive the maximum amount of sunlight. The more sunlight they receive, the more energy they can generate. Ideally, it would help if you placed the solar panel directly toward the sun and then followed the sun's movement across the sky.

Unfortunately, solar trackers that track the sun are prohibitively expensive for most home applications. It's generally cheaper to purchase a few more solar panels than a solar tracker's price; therefore, a compromise must be considered.

The sun should be at 90 degrees on solar panels for as long as possible at the time of day. In the US, most solar panels are located towards the equator (south) and at an angle between 30 and 45 degrees.

  • For homes in the northern hemisphere, solar panels should be facing south.
  • For homes in the southern hemisphere, solar panels should be facing north.

What is the best angle for Solar Panels at Home?

The best tilt angle isn't something everyone should attempt by themselves, especially when you are not an expert in the field. Since the sun's position changes location throughout the day, and solar panels on rooftops aren't designed to be moved after installation, it can be challenging to determine the proper solar panel's tilt angle. Most calculations are best left to professional solar panel designers and installers.

Tilt Angle vs Azimuth Angle

When it comes time to position solar panels, there are two angles to think about:

  • "Tilt Angle" - This is the angle vertical of solar panels. It is also referred to as the "Elevation Angle." The more slender the angle, the more "up" towards the sky they look.
  • "Azimuth Angle" is the horizontal angle solar panels have around the equator.

Solar panels located in the northern part of the hemisphere need to be facing south, and you might think that calculating the azimuth angle is easy - point them south! However, it's crucial to understand that solar panels should be angled toward the true south, not the reading of a magnetic south compass. Depending on the region of the United States you live in, the ideal azimuth angle can be at least 20 degrees from the magnetic south.

3 important Factors that Determine the best angle for solar Panels

Your roof, your location, and your weather determine the best angle for solar panels for your roof:

1. Your Location

Your latitude and longitude are vital in determining the best position for solar panels. The equator is where the sun shines directly overhead most of the year. Therefore, it is critical to direct your discussions toward that point.

  • The closer you are to the equator, the narrower the angle. If you lived near the equator, you could direct your solar panels upwards and be optimized to generate solar power.
  • The further you are away from the equator, the steeper angle is required. If you were living on the Poles. In that scenario, you'd need to position your solar panels towards the sky to maximize your power from solar.

Your location also affects how many sun-beam hours your home is exposed to. We're not talking about the time between sunset and sunrise. Consider the peak sun's hours as the period every day when the sun shines at its brightest.

2. Your Roof

To maximize the amount of energy you produce, your home's roof must meet a few essential specifications. Experts from a reputable solar power company will examine your roof and advise you which of these three elements could influence the ideal angle of your solar panels.

  • The angle of your roof

    • Every home is unique, as is each roof. From the steep roofs on a snow-covered cabin to the flat roofs of a modern desert house, many angles for roofs are to be considered. Based on the roof's angle, the solar installer might require extra support structures to alter the angle of the solar panels. These calculations could become more complex if the roof has multiple angles, corners, or forms.

  • The Shape of Your Roof

    • The form of your roof is a crucial aspect of solar panel installation. Gable or hip roofs are typically considered the best for solar panels, offering optimal sunlight exposure. However, the more decorative elements are on your roof, the more challenging it will be to determine the best place for your solar panels and the angle they should be. This should be left to skilled solar experts.

  • The Shading on Your Roof

    • Solar panels generate electricity by absorbing sunlight; anything that blocks them from the sun's rays will drastically reduce their power generation. Positioning your solar panels so that the slightest shade can be reflected on them is essential, as even minor dye can harm the solar system. To avoid shading, you must consider every hour of the day and throughout the seasons to ensure that no object in the vicinity could cause shadows to your roof when the sun moves from east to west.

3. Your Seasonal Weather

Your position on the Earth will determine the sunlight you will likely receive during a particular season. Because the sun's height varies from one location to another and season to season, solar seasonality significantly influences the optimal angles of solar panels. For instance, suppose your area is subject to fog in the morning. In that scenario, you should angle the panels to maximize later afternoon production.
