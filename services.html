Grants are funds that are used to encourage renewable energy sources that do not require payment back. It is important to note that the U.S. government is committed to renewable energy initiatives and for good reason. A few of the ways that it invests to benefit the United States as a whole are:

  • Removing the use of fossil fuels that are finite and assisting the nation to be energy independent
  • Enhancing the environmental quality by cutting CO2 emissions

The federal tax credit for investment (ITC) is without doubt the best solar incentive. The ITC provides 30 percent of the amount you spent on the solar installation on federal income taxes. In lieu of deducting that reduces your taxable income (as is the case with all charitable contributions you make throughout an entire year) A tax credit directly offsets the amount you'd otherwise be owed in taxes. Also, instead of being taxed on a lower amount of income, the federal ITC reduces the amount you owe in taxes. It may even return to you as a tax refund to the IRS.

STATE TAX CREDITS

Certain states provide additional tax incentives for the installation of solar photovoltaic panels that function as the federal ITC does, but with regard to your state tax obligations. The amounts are different for each state, but when combined together with federal ITC they can make a difference!

CASH REBATES

Certain states, municipalities, and utility companies offer upfront incentives for the installation of solar panels. Rebates for solar panels are usually only offered for a specific period of time, and then disappear after a number of solar panels have been installed in your area.
